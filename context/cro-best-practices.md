## Content Structure for Conversion

### Problem-Aware Content
**Goal**: Educate about the problem
**CTA**: Download guide, read related article
**Example**: "7 Signs Your Current Solution Isn't Working"

### Solution-Aware Content
**Goal**: Present your solution
**CTA**: Start free trial, schedule demo
**Example**: "How [Product] Solves [Problem]"

### Product-Aware Content
**Goal**: Close the sale
**CTA**: Buy now, upgrade, contact sales
**Example**: "[Product] vs. [Competitor]: Detailed Comparison"

## Call-to-Action (CTA) Guidelines

### CTA Placement
- **Above the fold**: One primary CTA
- **After key sections**: Contextual CTAs
- **End of content**: Strong final CTA
- **Sidebar**: Persistent offer

### CTA Copy
**Strong Verbs**:
- Get
- Start
- Try
- Download
- Learn
- See

**Weak Verbs to Avoid**:
- Submit
- Click
- Enter
- Continue

### CTA Examples by Content Type

**Educational Post**:
- "Get the free guide to [topic]"
- "Download our [resource] checklist"
- "See how [product] can help"

**Product Post**:
- "Start your free 14-day trial"
- "See [feature] in action"
- "Get started for free"

**Comparison Post**:
- "Try [product] free for 14 days"
- "See why [X] companies chose us"
- "Schedule a personalized demo"

## Conversion Elements

### Trust Signals
- Customer logos
- Testimonials
- Case study results
- Expert endorsements
- Security badges
- User counts

### Urgency & Scarcity
- Limited-time offers
- Countdown timers
- Limited availability
- Seasonal messaging

### Risk Reversal
- Free trials
- Money-back guarantees
- No credit card required
- Easy cancellation

## Landing Page Elements

### Above the Fold (First Screen)
1. Compelling headline
2. Supporting subheadline
3. Primary CTA button
4. Trust indicator
5. Hero image/video

### Body Content
1. Problem agitation
2. Solution presentation
3. Feature/benefit sections
4. Social proof
5. FAQ
6. Final CTA

### Below the Fold
1. Detailed features
2. More testimonials
3. Comparison table
4. Additional CTAs

## Content Types & Conversion Goals

| Content Type | Primary Goal | Secondary Goal | CTA |
|--------------|--------------|----------------|-----|
| How-To Guide | Educate | Capture email | Download PDF |
| Case Study | Build trust | Demo request | See more results |
| Comparison | Help decide | Free trial | Start trial |
| Product Update | Announce | Upgrade | See new features |
| Industry News | Engage | Stay top-of-mind | Subscribe |

## Common CRO Mistakes

1. **Too many CTAs** — One primary action per page
2. **Weak copy** — Use strong, specific language
3. **Hidden CTAs** — Make them prominent
4. **No social proof** — Include trust signals
5. **Ignoring mobile** — Optimize for all devices
6. **No urgency** — Create reasons to act now
7. **Generic offers** — Make CTAs specific and relevant

## CRO Checklist

Before publishing:
- [ ] Primary CTA is clear and prominent
- [ ] CTA uses action-oriented language
- [ ] Trust signals included
- [ ] Content matches search intent
- [ ] Mobile experience tested
- [ ] Page load time optimized
- [ ] Form fields minimized (if applicable)
- [ ] Risk reversal included (guarantee, trial, etc.)
