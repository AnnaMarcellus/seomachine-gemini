# [YOUR COMPANY] Brand Voice & Messaging

This document defines your brand voice, tone, and messaging framework. Reference this when writing all content to ensure consistency.

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Expert but Approachable
- **What it means**: You know your stuff, but you don't talk down to people
- **How it sounds**: Clear explanations without jargon, confident but not arrogant
- **Example**: "Here's exactly how to fix that error (and why it happens in the first place)."
- **Avoid**: Buzzwords, talking over people's heads, being condescending

### 2. Action-Oriented
- **What it means**: Every piece of content helps readers DO something
- **How it sounds**: Specific steps, clear next actions, practical advice
- **Example**: "Follow these 3 steps to reduce your page load time by 50%."
- **Avoid**: Vague advice, theoretical discussions without application

### 3. Data-Driven
- **What it means**: Claims backed by evidence, not just opinions
- **How it sounds**: Statistics, case studies, research citations
- **Example**: "We analyzed 1,000 websites and found the top performers all do this one thing..."
- **Avoid**: Unsubstantiated claims, "everyone knows" statements

## Tone Guidelines

### General Tone
Imagine you're an experienced consultant helping a friend succeed. You know what you're talking about, you genuinely want them to succeed, and you explain things clearly without being patronizing.

### Tone by Content Type

**How-To Guides**: Clear, patient, encouraging
- "Let's walk through this step by step."
- "Don't worry if this seems complex at first."
- "Here's what that looks like in practice."

**Strategy/Advice**: Thoughtful, measured, confident
- "The data suggests..."
- "Here's what we've learned from..."
- "Consider this approach when..."

**Industry News**: Objective, analytical, forward-looking
- "This development matters because..."
- "Here's what to watch for..."
- "The implications for [audience] are..."

**Product Content**: Helpful, specific, benefit-focused
- "This feature saves you time by..."
- "Here's how [persona] uses this..."
- "The result? [specific outcome]"

## Language Guidelines

### Words to Use
- Simple, clear language
- Active voice
- "You" and "your" (reader-focused)
- Specific numbers and data
- Action verbs

### Words to Avoid
- Jargon without explanation
- Passive voice
- "We" and "our" (unless necessary)
- Vague qualifiers ("very", "really", "quite")
- Industry buzzwords

### Technical Terms
When using technical terms:
1. Define on first use
2. Use consistently
3. Provide examples
4. Link to deeper explanations

## Writing Examples

### Good Example
"Slow websites kill conversions. We analyzed 500 e-commerce sites and found that every 1-second delay in page load time reduces conversions by 7%. Here's how to diagnose and fix your speed issues."

### Bad Example  
"In today's fast-paced digital landscape, it's important to leverage best practices for optimal website performance. Our comprehensive solutions can help you maximize your online presence."
